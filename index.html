Repayment

A repayment mortgage includes both interest and principal

payments. Over time, the outstanding balance decreases

until the loan is fully paid off.

Formula for Monthly Payment

\( M = P \frac{r(1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n - 1} \)

where:

  • M = Monthly payment
  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12)
  • n = Number of payments (loan term in years multiplied by 12)

Example Calculation:

  • Principal (P): $200,000
  • Annual interest rate: 5% (0.05)
  • Mortgage term: 30 years

Convert annual interest rate to monthly:

\( r = \frac{0.05}{12} = 0.004167 \)

Convert mortgage term to months:

\( n = 30 \times 12 = 360 \)

Plug these values into the formula:

\( M = 200{,}000 \frac{0.004167(1 + 0.004167)^{360}}{(1 + 0.004167)^{360} - 1} \)

Calculate:

\( M \approx 200{,}000 \frac{0.004167 \times 4.46774}{3.46774} \)

\( M \approx 200{,}000 \times 0.001208 = \$1{,}073.64 \)

Interest Only

An interest-only mortgage involves paying only

the interest on the principal balance for a set period.

The principal remains unchanged during this period.

Formula for Monthly Payment

\( M = P \times r \)

where:

  • M = Monthly payment
  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12)

Example Calculation:

  • Principal (P): $200,000
  • Annual interest rate: 5% (0.05)
Convert annual interest rate to monthly:

\( r = \frac{0.05}{12} = 0.004167 \)

Plug these values into the formula:

M = 200,000 \times 0.004167 = $833.33
